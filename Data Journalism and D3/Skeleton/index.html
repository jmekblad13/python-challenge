Lose Weight, Make More Money!

Based on statistics from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and the United States Census Bureau, the percentage of residents in a state with a normal body mass index (18.5-24.9%) is correlated with mean household income (correlation = .644526). As you can see in the chart above, states with higher percentages of residents with a normal BMI generally have higher mean household incomes. This suggests that getting in shape could increase your annual household income. So, please stay tuned for a word from one of our health food sponsors!
